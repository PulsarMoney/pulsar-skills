# Examples

Use these examples to keep the rewrite grounded and concrete.

## 1. Replace the slogan

**Input**
Each side built half the product. Pulsar Money is building the whole thing.

**Output**
Traditional fintech apps offer strong user experience but limited crypto functionality. Crypto-native apps offer on-chain assets and yield but usually lack integrated fiat balances, cards, and multi-currency accounts. Pulsar combines both in one product.

## 2. Replace the label with the mechanism

**Input**
Pulsar will be Arc-native, with Arc serving as the foundational and default chain across the platform.

**Output**
Pulsar uses Arc as its default chain for balances, earn products, and settlement.

## 3. Say what happens to cross-chain deposits

**Input**
Every single token that the user sees will be placed on Arc even if the user deposits them from other chains.

**Output**
Assets deposited from other chains are bridged into Arc and represented there by default.

## 4. Replace the metaphor

**Input**
Arc becomes the invisible default settlement layer.

**Output**
Users do not choose a chain. The app routes assets to Arc by default and shows balances there.

## 5. Define the stack

**Input**
Pulsar deploys the full neobank stack on Arc.

**Output**
Pulsar runs accounts, swaps, cards, yield products, and B2B payments on Arc.

## 6. Replace mind-reading with a thesis

**Input**
People who hold crypto don't want another crypto app with a card attached.

**Output**
Pulsar is designed for users who want a fintech app with integrated crypto balances, swaps, cards, and yield.

## 7. Soften the absolute

**Input**
Every card swipe settles on Arc.

**Output**
The card draws from Arc-based balances, with Arc handling the asset-side settlement flow.

## 8. Narrow the risk claim

**Input**
Cross-chain liquidity without bridge risk.

**Output**
CCTP moves native USDC and EURC between supported chains without wrapped-asset bridge risk.

## 9. Remove enterprise fog

**Input**
This is the consumer distribution layer that converts Arc's institutional-grade infrastructure into everyday financial activity.

**Output**
Pulsar brings Arc into consumer flows such as deposits, swaps, card spending, yield, and cross-border transfers.

## 10. Make compliance claims sound adult

**Input**
This directly addresses the requirements of Pulsar's European banking and e-money license partners.

**Output**
This helps meet the screening and audit requirements of Pulsar's banking and e-money partners.

## Quick checklist

- Does the line describe a mechanism or just a vibe?
- Is any absolute stronger than the evidence?
- Am I repeating the same claim in new words?
- Did I claim to know what all users want?
- Can a skeptical reader ask "how?" and win immediately?
- Can I swap one prestige word for a concrete detail?
- If I cut 25% of this paragraph, does the meaning improve?
